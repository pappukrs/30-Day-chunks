Day 6 — Saying "I Don't Know" Confidently
Yaar yeh day sabse important hai practically —
Sabse bada fear hota hai — "agar mujhe answer nahi pata toh log kya sochenge?"
But reality yeh hai 👇

Confidently saying "I'm not sure" = more respect, not less.

Senior engineers yeh baar baar bolte hain. Juniors jo fake karte hain — woh pakde jaate hain.

Pehle Chunks Warm Up — 3 baar bolo
"I'm not 100% sure but I think..."
"I'll need to check that"
"Let me get back to you on that"
"I'm not entirely sure — let me verify"
"That's a good question — I'll look into it"
"I think it's X but don't quote me on that"

Yeh Phrases 3 Situations Mein Use Hoti Hain
Situation 1 — Partial knowledge hai
"I'm not 100% sure but I think the issue 
is coming from the config file"
Situation 2 — Bilkul pata nahi
"That's a good question — 
I'll need to check that and get back to you"
Situation 3 — Meeting mein suddenly koi question aaye
"I think it's X, but let me verify 
and confirm by end of day"

Why This Feels Hard — Honest Baat
Teen reasons hote hain —
1. School conditioning
Hume school mein sikhaya gaya — "answer pata hona chahiye" — nahi pata toh sharam. But real work mein nobody knows everything.
2. Hindi mein yeh easy hai
"Bhai pata nahi yaar" — easily bol lete ho. English mein same confidence nahi aati. So practice se aayegi.
3. Silence ka dar
Jab pata nahi hota — hum ya toh chup ho jaate hain ya random kuch bol dete hain. Yeh phrases us silence ko fill karte hain confidently.

Aaj Ka Real Exercise
Main tujhe 5 surprise questions poochunga — tech related —
Kuch ka answer tujhe pata hoga, kuch ka nahi —
Rule sirf yeh hai:

Pata hai → answer do
Nahi pata → aaj ke phrases use karo, no freezing, no silence
